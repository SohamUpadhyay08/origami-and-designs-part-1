Interesting facts about CAMEL

  • Camels can reach 7 feet in height (at the hump) and weigh up to 1500 pounds.
  • Camels can survive in harsh environments. They can survive without food and water for long.
  • Camels can drink nearly 200 litres of water in one day.
  • Camels have an extraordinary eyesight that allows them to recognise threats as far as 3-4 km.
  • Camels can live for 40 to 50 years.

Interesting facts about CHAMELEON

  • Chameleon are repiles that are part of the iguna suborder.
  • Most chameleons have prehensile tail that they use to trap among tree branches.
  • There are around 160 species of chameleon.
  • Chameleons have amazing eyes. The bulging upper and lower eyelids are joined and the pupil peaks out from a pinhole sized gap.
  • Chameleons are not deaf but they do not actually have ear openings.

Interesting facts about CICADA

  • Cicadas are separated into two classifications, annual cicadas and periodic cicadas.
  • Annual cicadas emerge every year, while periodic cicadas emerge every 13 to 17 years.
  • Periodic cicadas are only found in the United States of America.
  • Cicadas are known for the loud sounds they can produce.
  • Periodic cicadas use a method called predator satiation to ensure their survival.

Interesting facts about PANDA

  • In China, they are known as ‘da xiong mao’, which means giant bear cat.
  • Male pandas are called boars. Female pandas are called sows. Their young are known as cubs.
  • Cubs are born blind, but sadly survival rate is low.
  • The fur is patterned to break up its outline as camouflage in forests to protect itself.Their fur is also waterproof.
  • 99% of a Giant Panda’s diet is made up of branches, stems, and leaves of the bamboo plant.

Interesting facts about PIGEON

  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  • Pigeons and humans have lived in close proximity for thousands of years. The first recordings of this date back to Mesopotamis, modern Iraq, in 3000bc.
  • Pigeons are fed by many members of different religions including Muslims, Hindus and Sikhs for spiritual reasons.
  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph. The fastest recorded speed is 92.5 mph.

Interesting facts about BEAR

  • The most accurate way to determine the age of a bear is to count the rings in a cross section of its tooth root under a microscope.
  • Bears live as long as 30 years in the wild. One captive brown bear lived to the age of 47.
  • Bears can run up to 40 miles per hour, fast enough to catch a running horse.
  • Sun bears have the longest claws of any bear. They also have the longest tongues, which can reach 9.8" long.
  • Black bears are not always black. They come in a rainbow of colors from black to reddish brown (cinnamon bears) to light brown to white.
